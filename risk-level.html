Knowing Your Risk Level

One of the foundational principles of finance is that risk and return are directly proportional. If your investment objective is focused on higher returns, you'll need to assume more risk to achieve those returns.

Risk tolerance describes the amount of risk an individual is willing to take on to achieve a particular level of return. Your own personal risk tolerance is one of the most important factors to consider when it comes to establishing your investment objectives and making investment decisions.

Know your risk tolerance level by finishing the test.

The Test:

Answer the following questions to gain a better understanding of where you fall on the risk tolerance spectrum.

  1. 1. I am willing to accept above average risk to achieve above average investment returns.

  2. 2. I am willing to hold an investment rather than sell it if I expect it to rebound within 12 months, even though it has lost value over the past year.

  3. 3. I am comfortable investing in the stock market.

  4. 4. I would rather invest in a single stock than a diversified portfolio.

  5. 5. I am willing to accept short-term losses for the potential of long-term gains.

  6. 6. I am comfortable with fluctuations in the value of my investments.

  7. 7. I prioritize the preservation of my initial investment over the potential for significant growth.

  8. 8. I am comfortable with investing a significant portion of my portfolio in high-risk assets with the potential for high returns.

  9. 9. I am willing to invest in significant risk to potentially achieve significant returns.

  10. 10. I am willing to take on substantial investment risk to potentially achieve substantial investment returns.
